My Favorite Recipe: Watermelon Sinigang!

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 Kilo of Flank steak
  • 1/2 Kilo of Beef Short Ribs
  • 1/4 Kilo of Ripe Watermelon
  • Sinigang Mix
  • 5 pcs. Okra
  • 1 bundle Kangkong
  • 2 pcs. Native Tomatoes
  • 1 Onion
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 1 Knorr Beef Cube
  • Water

Cooking Tools:

  • Cutting Board
  • Knife
  • Pot or Pressure Cooker
  • Strainer
  • Measuring Spoon
  • Bowl
  • Cup
  • Spoon
  • Fork
  • Plate or Bowl for Serving

Instructions:

  1. Cut up all the vegetables and watermelon into bite sized pieces so that the ingredients will be cooked evenly.
  2. In a pot, place the cuts of meat and boil the meat for 15 minutes so that unwanted fat is removed from beef.
  3. After the 15 minutes is done, rinse the beef again under running water, and in a pressure cooker place the beef,
    tomatoes, onion, ½ tablespoon of salt, ¼ tablespoon of pepper, and enough drinking water to cover the meat.
    Now, the reason I am using a pressure cooker is because it’s faster and “more” efficient, but this is merely optional.
    You can use a pressure cooker for 30 minutes or use a pot and cook the beef for 3 hours.
  4. The most important part of watermelon sinigang is watermelon.
    And you must have a ripe watermelon or else the broth might taste bitter.
    While the meat is cooking, in another pot boil the watermelon with 4 cups of drinking water
    for 10 minutes to soften up the watermelon.
    We do this so that we can remove the seeds easier.
  5. Once the watermelon is done boiling, separate the seeds from the juice and set it aside.
  6. When the 30 minutes is done, remove all the air inside before opening the pressure cooker to avoid accidents.
    Once the air is removed, uncover the pressure cooker and place the watermelon juice inside.
  7. Once you have the watermelon inside the pressure cooker, mix it with kangkong and okra, and boil it for 10 minutes.
    After the timer is done, add Sinigang mix and Knorr beef cubes.
    Make sure all the sinigang mix and beef cubes are dissolved and after that turn off the heat.

Once it is all done, the finished product should smell like sinigang, but when you taste it,
it has a fruity and slightly sweet taste that complements the sourness of the sinigang mix.
The broth should also have a nice gold and reddish color with floating watermelon pieces.
Serve in a bowl and this is usually eaten with rice. Enjoy!
